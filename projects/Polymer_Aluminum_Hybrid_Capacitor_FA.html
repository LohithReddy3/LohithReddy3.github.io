Polymer Aluminum Hybrid Electrolytic Capacitor — Failure Analysis

FMEA, optical microscopy, X‑ray, IR testing, and controlled cross‑sectioning led to a copper‑contamination root cause and corrective actions.

Objectives & Scope

  • Determine root cause for field failures (venting/shorts) across loose and PCB‑mounted units.
  • Quantify contribution of termination design and soldering process to conductive bridge formation.
  • Deliver corrective actions and inspection criteria.

Equipment

  • Optical microscope (1000×), micro‑positioning stage, polarizers.
  • X‑ray radiography for roll/termination screening.
  • LCR/IR bench with guarded fixtures; environmental control.
  • Sectioning saw, epoxy mounts, progressive polishing to 0.05 µm.

Procedure

  1. FMEA → map symptoms to mechanisms; prioritize with RPN.
  2. IR screening and X‑ray NDT scan.
  3. Termination cross‑section; progressive polish; document roll integrity.
  4. Correlate pre/post IR with microscopy/X‑ray.

Results & Observations

  • Anode structure variations at termination (thickness non‑uniformity, edge burrs).
  • Copper particles on roll surfaces and within termination compound.
  • IR decline after thermal exposure on parts with contamination sites.
Root Cause: Copper contamination bridging roll layers near termination; high‑energy soldering transferred copper into termination compound, enabling latent shorts → venting.

Recommendations

  • Particulate cleanliness gates at termination; ban copper tools in critical zones.
  • Constrain solder energy profile & tip metallurgy; pilot build section checks.
  • Incoming inspection: X‑ray sampling for roll anomalies/contamination.

Deliverables

  • FA report with cross‑section imagery and IR/X‑ray correlation.
  • Process control plan and inspection checklist.
