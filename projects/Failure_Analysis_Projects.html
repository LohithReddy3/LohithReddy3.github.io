Failure Analysis of Capacitors & Resistors

Root cause investigations of Polymer Aluminum Hybrid Electrolytic Capacitors, MLCCs, and SMD Resistors with cross-sectional and non-destructive methods.

Polymer Aluminum Hybrid Electrolytic Capacitor Failure Analysis

  • Performed FMEA on loose and mounted capacitors using optical microscopy, X-ray, and insulation resistance.
  • Developed a cross-sectioning procedure preventing roll damage; identified Anode structure variations at the termination.
  • Found copper particles on the roll as the root cause of failure. Copper was also present in termination material — likely transferred during high-energy soldering, leading to shorting and explosion.
  • Documented end-to-end processes, methodologies, and results with full traceability, completing deliverables in 2 months.

MLCC Failure Analysis

  • Performed electrical isolation on PCB and analysis with optical microscopy and insulation resistance.
  • Applied non-destructive methanol test, followed by cross-sectioning, revealing cracks as the root cause of failure.

SMD Resistor Failure Analysis

  • FMEA literature review identified corrosion at termination due to sulfur as a major failure mechanism.
  • Conducted electrical analysis, verifying solder joint trace issues with microscopy and FTIR.
  • Performed composition & surface analysis (EDS); corrosion was evident in cross-sections at termination.
  • Observed gaps at termination causing failures with high resistance values during electrical testing.
